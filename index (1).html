Research Statement

I actively conduct research in AI-driven biomedical signal processing, brain–computer interfaces, and intelligent medical decision-making. My work focuses on developing efficient, interpretable, and real-time decision support systems for healthcare applications. I design novel feature representation and multimodal fusion strategies to model complex interactions in non-stationary biosignals such as EEG and sEMG. I explore attention-based deep learning, time–frequency analysis, and ensemble frameworks to extract clinically meaningful patterns from uncertain data. I also develop lightweight models suitable for point-of-care deployment. Currently, I work on hybrid BCI systems for effective decision communication. My long-term goal is to build trustworthy AI systems for neurological healthcare and assistive technologies.
